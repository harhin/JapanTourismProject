Sightseeing Locations


Japan is a country known for a diverse and multitude of things! From beautiful natural scenery and serene and spiritual temples to the towering skyscrapers of urban metropolitan Japan, there is something for every visitor!

Ghibli Museum

The Ghibli Museum is a museum dedicated to the Japanese animation studio, Studio Ghibli, known for some of the most iconic movies to come out of the medium! It features exhibits from scenes and characters of their movies, think of it like a Japanese DisneyLand, though there is also one of those.

Tokyo Tower

Located in Tokyo, It is the 2nd largest building in Tokyo and is one of the major communications towers, built in 1958. It is similar in design to the eiffel tower, as it follows a lattice design.

Tokyo Skytree

It is currently the tallest building in all of Japan and the second tallest (formally the tallest before the Burj Khalifa) in the whole world. If your looking for an amazing view of the city and nearby sea, then this is the place for you! Though a warning, it may take some time to get all the way up there.

Mount Fuji

The Serene and beautiful Mount Fujiis one of Japan’s most famous and recognizable landmarks, to the point where the word Japan could be associated with the large volcano! Beware though, if you wish to see Mt. Fuji with your own eyes, you better wake up early, as many times clouds cover most of the mountain! That’s just how tall it is.

Japanese Temples & Shrines

If your interested in seeing traditional Japanese architecture, then look no further than the many temples and shrines dedicated to the Shinto and Buddhist religions of Japan! With many sites located all across Japan, you can easily find a temple or shrine by just asking a local or by looking online, and it won’t even be that far of a trip too (it tends to be free too). If you would like to see some of the more famous (and at times, larger) temples and shrines then here is a list of 5 of some of the most famous sites:

  • Meiji Jingu Shrine
  • Itsukushima Shrine
  • Nikko Toshugu Shrine
  • Tsurugaoka Hachimangu
  • Fushimi Inari Taisha

Japanese Castles

Many of the famed castles such as Osaka Castle or Himeji Castle With tall central keeps and angled stone walls, the castles of Japan and it’s samurai lords are some of the most unique and beautiful structures that can be seen in Japan while still being formidable in its defence during Japan’s wars of the past. There are also some of the most extravagant buildings constructed by the Japanese, with some being gilded in huge amounts of gold! Here is our top 5 list of Japanese Castles!

  1. Osaka Castle
  2. Himeji Castle
  3. Nagoya Castle
  4. Matsumoto Castle
  5. Kumamoto Castle
